Technical Writing & Comparative Analysis

Java vs. Python: A Language Comparison

A side-by-side breakdown of two of the most widely used object-oriented languages — how their typing systems, execution models, performance, and concurrency support differ, and what that means for choosing the right one for a real project rather than the most familiar one.

Reflection

The same OOP ideas — classes, inheritance, polymorphism — look genuinely different once they're expressed through each language's syntax and typing rules. Working through the comparison sharpened a skill this portfolio keeps coming back to: evaluating a tool on its technical trade-offs instead of defaulting to whichever one is more familiar.
